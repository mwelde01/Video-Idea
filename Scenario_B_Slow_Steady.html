AI as Normal Technology

Arvind Narayanan and Sayash Kapoor frame AI as normal technology rather than a "superintelligence." They argue its impact depends on slow diffusion and human control, not utopian or dystopian leaps. Policy should focus on resilience and sector-specific regulation.

The Simple Macroeconomics of AI

Daron Acemoglu predicts modest macroeconomic gains from AI, estimating a 10-year TFP increase under 0.7%. While AI targets diverse demographic groups, it likely widens the capital-labor gap without reducing inequality. Some growth may even be welfare-negative due to manipulative tasks.

The AI Revolution Wont' Happen Overnight

The AI revolution will be a slow, difficult process rather than an instant shift. Companies often misjudge ROI and ignore implementation hurdles. Success requires moving beyond generative hype to focus on specialized applications, proprietary data, and systemic integration.
